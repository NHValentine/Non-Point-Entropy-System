#### The Concept...





The original idea was pretty basic.  Dice math!



Roll a bunch of ten sided dice on their own little tables and take each result as a digit in a string. But the problem I had was that I didnt want just the digits to be random, I wanted the length of the output to be random too.  So I started playing with "magic" dice instead.  "Magic" 11 sided dice, where 0-9 places the digit, but if it hits 10 then \**poof*\* it never existed. Only the potential.

**Thats a Non-Point.**



Do that 256 times at once and collapse the digits that survived. Now, up that to 12 sided dice with two non-points.  Then 13 with 3. etc...  Thats the basic mechanics behind NPES. I call that the "flush\_rate". And the resulting outputs I call "residuum" value.



When I started doing that millions of times per second, oddly enough the tables they're landing on start to vibrate. Sometimes making more dice vanish and sometimes less. But that vibration is completely dependant on the original number base you're counting in. Table 10 and table 100 are BUMPIN' dice off for no reason other than we're counting by tens.  Count by 12's then table 12 and table 144 do it. Every base I tested shows effects on the outputs that are measurable and repeatable at their own base powers.



But then I started combining the base's and letting them run at the same time.  I tried alternating 10 and 12 first.  10, 12, 10, 12... Well, base 10's vibrations effect base 12's rolls.  And base 12's vibrations effect base 10's rolls.  The resulting rolls arent base 10's or base 12's alone.  Together they make some other third oddly shaped dice with different bases on different faces. Sometimes one, sometimes the other, sometimes neither and sometimes BOTH. It's like two kids jumping on a trampoline together. The resulting bounces arent from either kid, they come from "the kids". Sometimes you fly extra high and sometimes you just twist an ankle.



And the final test. The "orbit" test?  That's what happens when you let identical twins jump in perfect sync with one another AND the trampoline.



Thats the simple-ish metaphor version.
