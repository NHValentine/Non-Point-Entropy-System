##### Figure Descriptions

All of the graphs were made with raw data (included in this repo) on observable. The two GIF animations were also made directly from those graphs. 



###### Fig. 1

Negative base 10 in 2048 bit space. All 78 distributions overlaid into one graph.



###### Fig. 2

Base 10 and base 12 convergence dynamics in 256 bit space. For all graphs blue is base 10, yellow is base 12, and green is the combined. (a) all 51 flush\_rates over laid showing the full sweep. (b) Flush\_rate 19. Both distributions seperate with distinct peaks. (c) Flush\_rate 29.  This is the convergence zone with the valley between the distributions. (d) Flush\_rate 39.  The fully merged config with over lapping peaks.

(please also see the included GIF. Again, made from raw data and observable graphs, its the full sweep)



###### Fig 3

Base 10 and base negative 3, all 51 flush rates over laid.



###### Fig. 4

Stable "orbital" dynamics in dual negative base 4. For all the graphs one base -4 is blue and the other is yellow, while green is the combined. (a) Pulse 25, distance 32. This shows the approach during the initial convergence. (b) pulse 45, distance 0. This just one of many large amplitutde resonances during the damping cycle. (c) Pulse 46, distance 18. The system at maximum seperation after intial convergence. (d) Pulse 50, distance 0.  Settled zero crossing. The system phase locks by pulse 31, shows damping / resonance over the first few cycles, and continues drift free for 360 pulses regardless of the refresh rate. (tested at 12Hz, 60Hz, and 200Hz)

(Please also see the included GIF. Same as I mentioned above, it's made from the same raw data that's in this repo from graphs on observable. It represents the first 60 pulses / frames of the 360 pulse test.)



###### Fig. 5

This is from a comprehensive multi base test. Bases 10 and -2 were put through multiple sweeps in multiple configurations. This is all the flush\_rates from sweep 3 over laid.  Base -2 was locked at flush 3 while base 10 ascends. Even though base -2 has set parameters it shows modulation based on 10's position.
